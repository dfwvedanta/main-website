Retreat Overview

Our Fall Retreat with Swami Tyagananda was an unforgettable weekend of spiritual immersion, bringing together over 60 participants for three days of intensive study, meditation, and spiritual community. The retreat focused on practical applications of the Bhagavad Gita's timeless wisdom in modern life.

Swami Tyagananda, head of the Ramakrishna Vedanta Society of Boston and a renowned scholar, shared his deep insights into Sri Krishna's teachings with remarkable clarity and warmth. Attendees experienced a perfect balance of intellectual understanding and devotional practice throughout the weekend.

The retreat provided a rare opportunity to step away from daily routines and dive deep into spiritual practice, creating lasting memories and renewed commitment to the spiritual path.

About Swami Tyagananda

Swami Tyagananda is the head of the Ramakrishna Vedanta Society of Boston and has served the Ramakrishna Order for over four decades. A scholar of Sanskrit and comparative religion, he holds a doctorate in Religious Studies and has taught at Harvard University for many years.

Known for his erudition, humor, and ability to make ancient wisdom accessible to contemporary audiences, Swami Tyagananda has authored numerous articles and books on Vedanta philosophy. His teaching style combines scholarly rigor with practical wisdom, helping students understand not just the theory but the lived experience of spiritual truths.

Participants consistently praise his clarity of expression, depth of knowledge, and genuine care for each student's spiritual journey. His presence brings both inspiration and practical guidance for sincere seekers.

Key Teachings Explored

Throughout the retreat, Swami Tyagananda illuminated several core themes from the Bhagavad Gita:

  • Dharma and Purpose: Understanding our unique role and responsibilities in life
  • Karma Yoga: Transforming daily work into spiritual practice through selfless action
  • The Eternal Self: Realizing our true nature beyond body and mind
  • Equanimity: Maintaining inner peace amidst life's ups and downs
  • Integration of Paths: Harmonizing devotion (bhakti), knowledge (jnana), and action (karma)
  • Practical Application: Concrete ways to bring Gita's wisdom into modern challenges
