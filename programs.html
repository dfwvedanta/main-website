Activities

All programs are free and open to the public

The Ramakrishna Vedanta Society of North Texas offers a variety of programs to support spiritual seekers at every stage of their journey. Our activities combine traditional Vedantic teachings with practical guidance for modern life, creating a welcoming space for study, meditation, and community.

Whether you're new to Vedanta or an experienced practitioner, you'll find programs designed to deepen your understanding and practice. All activities are offered free of charge as a service to the community.

Weekly Programs

Sunday Service

Every Sunday, 11:00 AM - 12:30 PM

Our Sunday service is the heart of our community gathering. Each week begins with meditation, followed by devotional singing and an inspiring talk on Vedanta philosophy, spiritual practice, or the teachings of Sri Ramakrishna and Swami Vivekananda. The service concludes with arati (ritual worship) and prasad (blessed food).

Everyone is welcome regardless of religious background or level of spiritual experience. Come as you are, and feel free to participate as much or as little as you're comfortable with.

Special Programs

Spiritual Retreats

Intensive weekend or week-long retreats led by visiting monastics from the Ramakrishna Order. These immersive programs include extended meditation sessions, in-depth talks, and personal guidance.

Festival Celebrations

We celebrate major spiritual festivals including Sri Ramakrishna's birthday, Holy Mother's birthday, Swami Vivekananda's birthday, and traditional Hindu festivals with special worship and programs.

Guest Lectures

Throughout the year, we host visiting swamis, scholars, and spiritual teachers who offer specialized lectures on various aspects of Vedanta philosophy and spiritual practice.

Interfaith Programs

We actively participate in interfaith dialogue and host programs exploring the universal truths found in different religious traditions, embodying Vedanta's inclusive philosophy.

Children's & Youth Programs

Our children's program provides age-appropriate spiritual and cultural education in a warm, supportive environment. Through stories, activities, music, and discussion, children learn about universal values, character development, and the rich heritage of Vedanta.

The program meets regularly during the academic year, concurrent with Sunday services. Children are taught about the lives of great spiritual teachers, moral principles through stories, and simple meditation and yoga practices.
