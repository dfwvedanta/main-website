Our Sacred Emblem

Designed by Swami Vivekananda

This profound emblem was designed by Swami Vivekananda himself as a symbol of harmony and synthesis—the essence of his spiritual message. Each element represents a path to spiritual realization, and their union symbolizes the comprehensive approach of Vedanta.

The Wavy Waters

Karma Yoga – The path of selfless action and work. Through dedicated service without attachment to results, we purify the mind and realize the divine in action.

The Lotus

Bhakti Yoga – The path of devotion and love. Like the lotus that rises pure from muddy waters, devotion lifts us above worldly concerns to experience divine love.

The Rising Sun

Jnana Yoga – The path of knowledge and wisdom. The sun dispels darkness just as spiritual knowledge illuminates the truth of our divine nature.

The Encircling Serpent

Raja Yoga – The path of meditation and psychic control. The serpent represents the awakened Kundalini Shakti, the spiritual energy that leads to higher consciousness.

The Swan

Paramatman – The Supreme Self. The swan, known for its ability to separate milk from water, symbolizes the discrimination between the real and unreal, representing the ultimate goal of all spiritual paths.

तन्नो हंसः प्रचोदयात्
Tanno Hamsah Prachodayat

"May the Supreme Swan awaken our understanding"

Swami Vivekananda taught: "By the union of Karma, Jnana, Bhakti, and Yoga, the vision of the Paramatman is obtained."

This emblem reminds us that spiritual freedom is achieved not through one path alone, but through the harmonious integration of action, devotion, knowledge, and meditation.
