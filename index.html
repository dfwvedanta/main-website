A Living Spiritual Tradition

From 19th Century India to 21st Century North Texas

In 1893, Swami Vivekananda introduced Vedanta to America at the Parliament of World Religions in Chicago, proclaiming the universal message: "As the different streams having their sources in different places all mingle their water in the sea, so the different paths which people take lead to the same God."

This center continues that sacred mission, offering the timeless teachings of Sri Ramakrishna, Holy Mother Sarada Devi, and Swami Vivekananda. Here, spiritual seekers of all backgrounds discover their divine nature through meditation, study, and selfless service—transforming ancient wisdom into practical spirituality for modern life.

Guidelines for Visitors

Arrive Early

Please arrive 5-10 minutes before the scheduled time for seating and settling in.

Respectful Attire

Modest and comfortable clothing is recommended. Please remove shoes before entering the chapel.

Silence Phones

Please silence all electronic devices to maintain a peaceful atmosphere.

Children Welcome

Families with young children are welcome. We have a separate children's program available.

Accessible Parking

Ample parking is available. Accessible parking spots are located near the entrance.

Questions Welcome

Feel free to ask questions after the program. Our community is here to support your journey.
