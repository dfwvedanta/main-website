About Maha Shivaratri

Maha Shivaratri, "The Great Night of Shiva," is one of the most sacred nights in the Hindu spiritual calendar. It commemorates the divine marriage of Lord Shiva and Goddess Parvati, and is observed with fasting, prayer, meditation, and all-night vigil by devotees seeking spiritual liberation.

In the Vedantic tradition, Lord Shiva represents the Supreme Consciousness, the Absolute Reality beyond all dualities. Maha Shivaratri is an auspicious time for spiritual practices, as it is believed that the planetary positions on this night create a natural upsurge of energies conducive to spiritual awakening.

Join us for this special evening of worship, meditation, and spiritual discourse as we honor Lord Shiva and explore the profound symbolism and philosophy associated with this sacred observance.

Lord Shiva in Vedantic Philosophy

In Advaita Vedanta, Lord Shiva embodies several profound spiritual concepts:

  • Shiva as Pure Consciousness: The name "Shiva" means "the auspicious one" and represents the eternal, unchanging Consciousness that underlies all of existence.
  • The Destroyer and Transformer: Shiva destroys ignorance and ego, transforming the spiritual seeker through the dissolution of false identifications.
  • The Supreme Yogi: Depicted in deep meditation on Mount Kailash, Shiva exemplifies perfect stillness, detachment, and inner realization.
  • Beyond Duality: Shiva transcends all pairs of opposites - creation and destruction, form and formlessness, being and non-being.

The spiritual discourse during the program will explore these themes and their practical application in our spiritual journey toward self-realization.
