About This Service

Our annual Christmas Eve Service was a beautiful interfaith celebration honoring the birth and teachings of Jesus Christ. In the spirit of religious harmony that is central to the Ramakrishna-Vivekananda tradition, this special service brought together people of all faiths to celebrate the universal message of love, compassion, and spiritual awakening embodied by Jesus.

Sri Ramakrishna had profound reverence for Jesus Christ and experienced mystical visions of him during his spiritual practices. He taught that Christ represents the divine incarnation of universal love and that his teachings point to the same supreme Truth found in all religions.

The evening featured devotional singing, readings from the Gospels and Vedantic scriptures, meditation, and a spiritual discourse exploring the life and message of Jesus from the Vedantic perspective.

Jesus Christ in the Vedantic Tradition

The Ramakrishna-Vivekananda tradition has always held Jesus Christ in the highest reverence. Key perspectives include:

  • Divine Incarnation: Sri Ramakrishna recognized Jesus as an Avatar - a divine incarnation who came to awaken humanity to its spiritual nature.
  • Universal Love: Jesus's central teaching of love for God and neighbor mirrors the Vedantic principle of seeing the Divine in all beings.
  • Christ Consciousness: When Jesus proclaimed "I and my Father are one," he expressed the same non-dual realization taught in Vedanta - the unity of individual consciousness with the Supreme.
  • Path of Devotion: Jesus's life exemplifies Bhakti Yoga, the path of loving devotion to God and selfless service to humanity.
"I saw the picture of the Madonna in the parlour. It was made in such a way that the eyes seemed to follow you wherever you went.
