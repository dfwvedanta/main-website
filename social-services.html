Social Services

Daridra Narayana Seva - Service to God in the Poor

"Service to humanity is worship of God"

Following Swami Vivekananda's teaching that service to the poor and needy is service to God (Daridra Narayana Seva), we organize various programs throughout the year to serve our community. These initiatives embody the Ramakrishna Order's motto: "For one's own salvation and for the welfare of the world."

Swami Vivekananda taught us to see God in every being, especially in those who are suffering. Through selfless service, we not only help others but also purify our own hearts and move closer to spiritual realization.

Our Service Programs

Vivekananda Food Drive

The Vivekananda Food Drive consists of raising monetary donations for the Irving Cares Food Pantry in the name of the Ramakrishna Vedanta Society of North Texas.

You can contribute monetarily in any of these simple ways:

  • Check, payable to Ramakrishna Vedanta Society of North Texas — please write "Food Drive" or "Blanket Drive" in the memo line. If you'd like to support both, please use separate checks.
  • Electronic donation — scan the QR code on the donation sign holder on the bookshelf, which will open a Zeffy web-app. The app will allow you to select the cause for your donation. You can access the Zeffy donation link here.
  • Cash — you can also provide cash for this drive at the center.

Future Service Programs

We are continually exploring new ways to serve our community in the spirit of Karma Yoga (selfless service). Potential future programs may include:

  • Educational tutoring for underprivileged children
  • Senior citizen support and companionship programs
  • Health and wellness camps
  • Environmental cleanup initiatives
  • Disaster relief efforts

If you would like to volunteer or have ideas for community service projects, please reach out to us.
